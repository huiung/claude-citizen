CLAUDE CITIZEN — WIKI

An open-source attempt to finish Star Citizen before Star Citizen does. Built with Claude Fable 5.
▶ Play GitHub

GETTING STARTED

Pick a callsign and hit LAUNCH. You spawn in your hauler near the Meridian Refinery. No download, no account — your progress saves automatically (anonymously) and follows you back when you return. Grab a Pilot Code from the menu to carry your pilot to another device (one live session per code).

The one rule of this game: 60 seconds from click to flight. Everything that breaks that gets cut.

TRADING

The core loop. Dock at an outpost (Space) to open the station menu, then use the TRADE tab. Buy a commodity where it's cheap, sell where it's dear:

  • ORE — cheap at Helios Mining Colony, dear at Meridian Refinery
  • ALLOY — cheap at Refinery, dear at Colony

Both legs of the Colony ↔ Refinery run turn a profit, so you never fly home empty. Prices are dynamic — buying pushes a price up, selling pushes it down, and they drift back over time. Don't dump your whole hold at once.

MINING

Ore veins glow cyan and are scattered around you. Fly within range, hold Left-click, and the laser pulls ore straight into your hold. A vein holds only a fraction of a cargo hold and depletes as you mine — once it runs dry it vanishes and a fresh vein appears elsewhere nearby, so you're always prospecting and moving rather than parked on one rock. Upgrade your mining yield at a station to extract faster.

CRAFT & UPGRADES

At a station, the UPGRADES tab spends credits on cargo / speed / boost / mining-yield tiers — five tiers each, so there's always a bigger sink as your credits (and rank) grow. The SHIPYARD tab sells whole new hulls:

HullRole
HaulerBalanced starter — solid cargo and hull
FighterFast and agile, little cargo — for fighting pirates
MinerHuge hold, tanky, slow — for hauling and mining
InterceptorFastest, fragile — hit-and-run

RANKS

Every credit you earn counts toward your pilot rank — six tiers from Cadet to Admiral. A track on the HUD shows your progress to the next rank, a banner announces each promotion, and your rank is tagged next to your name on the leaderboard.

PIRATES & SAFE ZONES

Out in open space, pirate interceptors hunt you. Fight back with Right-click, watch your hull bar, and collect the bounty on a kill. The combat HUD helps: red brackets mark hostiles with their range, arrows at the screen edge point to threats behind or beside you, and a green lead indicator shows where to aim so your shots land. If you're losing, run for an outpost — safe zones around the stations keep hostiles off you. Die and you respawn at the origin, losing a little money but keeping your cargo.

QUANTUM TRAVEL

Space is vast. Press N to pick a destination — it cycles through the named planets (Mercury → Saturn), shown on the HUD with its distance. Or press M to open the Solar Atlas, a top-down map of the whole system — click any planet to set it as your destination at a glance. Then press J to spool the quantum drive: a couple seconds of charge, then you streak across the sector and drop out just short of the planet you chose. Press J again to cancel. Distant targets (Jupiter, Saturn) make for a long, cinematic cruise.

MULTIPLAYER

Other pilots share your sector in real time — you'll see their craft and callsigns. Hit Enter to chat; open it any time to scroll back through the session's history. A leaderboard ranks the top pilots by credits — on the landing screen and in-game with L. (Combat is currently solo; co-op fights are on the roadmap.)

TIPS

  • Toggle flight assist (V) off for true Newtonian drift — great for lining up a strafing run, harder to fly.
  • Mine ORE for free, then sell it at the Refinery to bootstrap your first upgrades.
  • Take delivery contracts (station tab) for guaranteed payouts on routes you're flying anyway.
  • Buy a Fighter before you go pirate-hunting; the Hauler can take hits but can't chase.
